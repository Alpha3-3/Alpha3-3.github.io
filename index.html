About

I’m an undergraduate researcher in the High-Performance Data-Intelligence Computing (HPDIC) Lab at the University of Washington. My research interests include applied mathematics, vector databases, information retrieval, large language models (LLMs), natural language processing (NLP), machine learning systems, and efficient algorithms for high-dimensional data.

Recent projects include ProHD, a projection-guided subset-selection algorithm for fast Hausdorff distance approximation (10–100× speedups with bounded error, supported by PNNL/DOE resources and large-scale clusters), and QPAD, an unsupervised, quantile-preserving dimension-reduction method that maximizes neighborhood margin to preserve approximate nearest-neighbor structure in high-dimensional vector search systems.
