About

I am an undergraduate researcher at the High-Performance Data-Intelligence Computing (HPDIC) Lab at the University of Washington. My interests lie in vector databases, approximate nearest neighbor search, dimensionality reduction, high-performance data systems, information retrieval, and machine learning systems.

My recent work includes ProHD, a projection-based method for fast Hausdorff distance approximation, and QPAD, an unsupervised dimensionality-reduction method designed to preserve nearest-neighbor structure in high-dimensional vector search. I am also working on directed hypergraph compression and reordering for large-scale data and ML systems.

Research Experience

Undergraduate Researcher — High-Performance Data-Intelligence Computing (HPDIC) Lab, University of Washington

Nov 2024 – Present · Seattle, WA · Advisor: Prof. Dongfang Zhao

ProHD: Projection-Based Hausdorff Distance Approximation (ICDM 2025) — proposed a projection-based subset-selection approach for fast Hausdorff distance estimation in high dimensions using centroid directions and principal components; achieved 10–100× speedups with controllable error on multiple datasets; supported by PNNL and evaluated on the Texas A&M ACES cluster and Chameleon testbed.

QPAD: Quantile-Preserving Approximate Dimension Reduction for Nearest Neighbors Preservation in High-Dimensional Vector Search (ICDE 2026) — developed an unsupervised dimensionality-reduction method for improving neighborhood preservation after DR in ANN systems; proved key mathematical properties and evaluated against common baselines on six datasets, showing consistent gains.

Directed Hypergraph Compression and Reordering — studying a 1D array storage format for directed hypergraphs to improve compression, cache locality, and runtime in ML and database workloads.

Techniques & Stack: Linux/VM; C/C++ (STL, OpenMP, Makefile); Python (multiprocessing, psutil, pandas, NumPy, SciPy); FAISS; matplotlib; mpi4py; PyOpenCL; Conda.
